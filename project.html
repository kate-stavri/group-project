Chemistry behind cosmetic products

The basics

  • Cosmetics are products designed to cleanse, protect and change the appearance of our bodies.
  • The key ingredients present in most cosmetics are water, emulsifiers, preservatives, thickeners, moisturisers, colours and fragrances.
  • Ingredients can be naturally occurring or artificial, but the impact upon health depends on the ingredients used.
  • The doses of potentially dangerous chemicals found in cosmetics are considered too small to pose a risk to human health.

Analysing 5 common cosmetics


How does my shampoo work?
Shampoos work on the same principle as detergents. By containing a substance called surfactant (which can be either SLS- sodium laureath sulfate or ammonium laureath sulfate) they help reduce the surface tension of water, making it more able to bind to oily molecules.
The surfactant molecule has a hydrophilic end, which dissolves in water and a hydrophobic one which interacts with greasy substances, like the sebum coating the hair. As a result, the oily molecules are forced to dissolve into water and rinsing leaves your hair clean!
Also, if the natural coating of the hair is removed, this would have undesirable effects on the aspect, so silicones are also added in order to keep your hair nice and shiny.

How does my cream keep my hands hydrated?
The main components of a moisturiser are: emollients, humectants and occlusive agents. Humectants (glycerol and urea) are the most important, as they can attract water from atmosphere and keep it bound to the corneum stratum. Emollients can help maintain a soft aspect of the skin, by smoothening the epidermic skin cells. Also, in order to prevent water loss from the tissue, occlusive agents like waxes and oils (which are insoluble in water) are added.
